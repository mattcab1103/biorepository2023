Biodiversity is linked to phylogeny. (1.2)

Biodiversity is linked to phylogeny and sets the stage for its development. But how? Biodiversity focuses on the variation between species while phylogeny describes the evolutionary history among groups of organisms. But what is phylogeny even anyway?

What is phylogeny?

Phylogeny is a part of systematics, a branch of biology focused on naming organisms(taxonomy) and seeing what their evolutionary relationships(phylogeny) are towards each other. Phylogeny also has phylogenetics, which is more based on the genetic evolutionary relationships.

Biodiversity, Evolution, and Phylogenetics

Biodiversity and phylogenetics are connected by way of evolution.

Evolution is descent with modification through natural selection. Biodiversity manages to supply natural selection, since biodiversity focuses on variation, thus creating a cycle.

Since evolution is connected to phylogenetics, and biodiversity is connected to evolution, thus phylogenetics and evolution are connected to one another.
