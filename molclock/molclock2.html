Molecular clocks help track evolutionary time. (2.1)

The goal of Biology is to understand the relationships among organisms, but this cannot be done since some organisms do not have a fossil record. With this, we need to set assumptions on how change occurs on the molecular level.

As said earlier, genomes mutation and thus species evolves, and creates an evolutionary lineage. Since each species is connected to each other, we can make a clock out of the amount of mutations versus the divergence in millions of years.

The speed in which the molecular clock evolves, depends on the neutral mutations. These neutral mutations are neither beneficial nor detrimental. As a result, the rate of evolution of those neutral mutations should be clock-like. The molecular clock ticks for every mutation.
